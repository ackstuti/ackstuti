A SHORT TRIP DOWN MEMORY LANE


Everything has a history. Here's a quick glimpse of some of our most memorable and cherished moments in the Institute's history.

The college (and the first classes) started inside the St Thomas Church building before shifting to a shade (pictured below)
 

The first ever structure to house the Institute's first classes is seen here under construction

 
 

Humble beginnings under a shade

 
 

The initial structure

 
 

Some of the Ufanisi Project pioneers seen here deliberating over the project's progress and way forward in 1998!

(From left to right): The late Mr McOne, former People's Warden Mr Moses Shaha, Vicar of St Thomas Church Ven. Harrison Chengo, Institute Manager Mr David Shaha, the Bishop of Mombasa Diocese the Right Reverend Julius Kalu and Church Elder Mr Jacob Saleri
 

The Award of Project Completion Certificate by CDTF (Community Development Trust Fund) officials, who construted two workshop buildings and purchased tailoring and carpentry tools for the college, is seen here being signed by Reverend Shadrack Thoya in the year 2000!

 
 

Let there be light! This is 2003. St Mary's Church of Everton, England's donation to aid in the installation of electricity couldn't have come at a better time. In the picture (from left to right) is Mr David Shaha, the then Institute Manager, and Mr Raymond Mghendi, the then Institute Treasurer. The donation, which amounted to Ksh 53,500.00 helped a great deal in the purchase of materials (seen here) and labour to install electricity in the Institute.
 

It is the year 2002. The Institute receives a batch of 15 computers, another huge and timely donation from Mr Brian and Mrs Margaret of the UK. The donation marked the beginning of training in computers.
 

Two more computers in 2005 by CDTF officials who are seen here doing a follow up of the project.

 
 
2011... College renovation in progress through the assistance of St Mary's Church of Everton, England.
