HISTORICAL BACKGROUND


The Ufanisi Training Institute was started in 1997 by two archdeaconries, Rabai and Kilifi, under the co-ordination of the then Sunday School co-ordinator Cannon Rev. Reuben Changawa.


The founding members if the two archdeaconries, consisting mainly of the youth, could not bear seeing their counterparts in the community heading for destruction in the areas of drug abuse, early pregnancy, robbery, among other social evils that arise from unemployment, idleness and deep-rooted poverty. To address challenges of unemployment, poverty and dropping out of schools among the youth, was one of the main reasons that led to the formation of the Institute, whose goals include:

1. To provide relevant vocational training to the youth and the community members in general to enbale them become self-reliant and improve their standards of living.

2. To take part in national building by providing the youth and community members with much-needed training in various disciplines.

3. To create employment opportunities for the community members.

4.To create awareness to the youth on the need to live an upright life.

5. To provide a professional ladder to the members of the community to progress towards higher rewarding careers.


The Ufanisi Project started conducting its training activities inside the church building with only one course, the Tailoring course. Its training activities were later on shifted to a newly constructed shade within the church compound.


In 1998, through the assistance of Mr Benjamin Mure (a former Institute B.O.G Chairman, who is currently the Kenyan Ambassador to Burundi), the Ufanisi Project got assistance from CDTF (Community Development Trust Fund) which saw the construction of workshop buildings and procurement of tailoring machines and carpentry tools at a value of Ksh 850,000.00.


In 2003, the institute's name was changed from the Ufanisi Youth Polytechnic to ACK Ufanisi Training Institute, so as to take in elder members of the society who would like to undertake trainings.


Installation of electricity to the college premises by St Mary's Church of Everton, England, took place in 2003.


The Insitute is currently registered by the Ministry of Higher Education and the Computer Society of Kenya and it is examined by KASNEB, ABMA (UK), KNEC, Kenya Industrial Training (KIT), among other examination bodies.
